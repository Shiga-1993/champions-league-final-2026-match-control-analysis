# 2026 Champions League Final Match Control Thread Draft

## Post 1

The 2026 Champions League final was a clean data story:

Paris Saint-Germain controlled the match, but Arsenal kept it 1-1 after 120 minutes.

Then Paris won 4-3 on penalties.

## Post 2

The basic control numbers were not close.

Paris had 75.3% possession, 21 shots, and 806 completed passes.

Arsenal had 24.7% possession, 7 shots, and 196 completed passes.

Image: `outputs/figures/team_control_snapshot.png`

## Post 3

This is the part I find interesting.

Paris had about 3x the possession share, 3x the shots, and 4.1x the completed passes.

But after extra time, the score was still 1-1.

Image: `outputs/figures/control_gap_vs_scoreline.png`

## Post 4

Timing explains some of it.

Havertz scored in the 6th minute. After that, Arsenal could protect a lead.

Paris kept the ball, but only got level from Dembele's penalty in the 65th minute.

Image: `outputs/figures/match_timeline.png`

## Post 5

So this final was not close in match control.

But it was very close in match outcome.

That gap is why one football stat is never enough.

## Post 6

The shootout made the difference.

Paris scored 4 of 5. Arsenal scored 3 of 5.

A match with a big control gap became a one-kick margin.

Image: `outputs/figures/penalty_shootout_sequence.png`

## Post 7

My takeaway:

Control gives a team more ways to win, but it does not remove variance.

In a final, one early goal can make even a dominant control profile look fragile.
